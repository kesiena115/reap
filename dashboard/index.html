Data Disclaimer

Data Disclaimer

The data provided are designed to help initiate and frame your thinking about data-driven decision making for your regional innovative and entrepreneurial ecosystem.

Much of the information provided is at a national level, but represents commonly used measures to evaluate regional innovation and entrepreneurship and to provide comparisons. The measures are not meant to be exhaustive, but rather informative examples of publicly available information to outside observers. Where possible, the data is for your specific region.

Not all measures will be relevant to every area, so we may emphasize different metrics based on the unique qualities of your individual region.

  • REAP Index

Developed by REAP faculty and scholars, the REAP index aims to overcome three challenges when assessing regional entrepreneurial performance. First, recognizing that firms matter in very different degrees, but that nonetheless each should be taken into account, the REAP index estimates the quality of firms and then develops aggregate statistics that weight different firms by their quality. Second, by using a quality-based approach, the REAP index methodology is able to incorporate all firms in the economy, and builds on a unique feature of the entrepreneurial process to anchor the data on which the index is built: registering a firm is an early and practical requirement for growth. And third, by having an estimate for each firm, in each cohort and address, the REAP index allows practitioners to dial into specific sub-regions, industry groups, or cohorts, to understand the distribution of entrepreneurial quality and quantity across the region geography and time.

The REAP index is built through a predictive model, calibrated at MIT, that uses business registration records to find features of firms at founding. These features include industry, founding team characteristics, corporate form, firm name, and intellectual property. We then provide different weights for each of these features to create a firm-level score, which we then aggregate across geography and time.This new approach offers practitioners a unique way to analyze, benchmark, and track IDE performance in their ecosystem.
